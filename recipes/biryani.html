1. Biryani


Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that is popular in Indian cuisine. It is made with basmati rice, meat (such as chicken, mutton, etc.) or paneer, and a blend of spices. The dish is often garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and boiled eggs.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or mutton, cut into pieces or paneer for veg biryani
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tsp biryani masala powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add the chicken or mutton pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  6. Stir in the yogurt, biryani masala powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the meat is well coated with the spices.
  7. Add the chopped coriander and mint leaves. Mix well.
  8. Add 4 cups of water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer until the meat is cooked through.
  9. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked. Drain the rice.
  10. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat in the pot. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  11. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot, garnished with fried onions and boiled eggs if desired.
