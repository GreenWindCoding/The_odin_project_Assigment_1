4. Masala Dosa


Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of a crispy, golden-brown fermented rice and lentil crepe filled with a spiced potato filling. It is typically served with coconut chutney and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew).

Ingredients:

  • For the Dosa Batter:
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed
  • For the Potato Filling:
    • 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
    • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • 8-10 curry leaves
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dosa Batter: Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight. Drain the water and grind them together to form a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The batter should be of pouring consistency. Add salt and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours.
  2. Prepare the Potato Filling: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, and curry leaves . Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown. Add turmeric powder and salt. Mix well.
  3. Add the mashed potatoes to the pan and mix thoroughly with the onion mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Make the Dosas: Heat a non-stick skillet or tawa over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy.
  5. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dosa. Fold the dosa over the filling to form a semi-circle or roll it up.
  6. Serve the masala dosa hot with coconut chutney and sambar on the side.
